
# LCG^2 (Rogue Jumper)

**Category:** Cryptography  
**Difficulty:** Medium/Hard  

## 📜 Scenario
You are tracking a "Rogue Jumper" starship. The ship's navigation drive is unstable, and its hyperspace jump intervals are controlled by an encrypted auxiliary circuit. To engage your tractor beam and capture the ship, you must accurately predict its next three jump coordinates. 

## 🔍 Technical Details
This challenge features a nested **Linear Congruential Generator (LCG)** architecture:

1. **Auxiliary LCG:** Operates on a small known modulus (m = 1031) with redacted parameters (a, c, seed). This LCG generates the "jump distance"—determining exactly how many states the Main LCG skips per turn.
2. **Main LCG:** Operates on a massive 128-bit prime modulus. Its state is advanced dynamically based on the output of the Auxiliary LCG.

Players are given exactly **20 queries** to either ping the current location (observe the Main LCG state) or attempt to lock on (predict the next 3 states). 

## 🎯 Objective
To get the flag, the player must:
1. Observe the outputs of the main 128-bit LCG.
2. Deduce the hidden parameters of the auxiliary LCG (modulus 1031).
3. Use the recovered parameters to accurately predict the next 3 coordinates generated by the main LCG.
